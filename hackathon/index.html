Official Prompt

Build an AI-powered STEM application that makes a meaningful difference for a community you know.

Identify a specific community, such as a school, neighborhood, patient population, underserved region, or nonprofit audience. Use AI to improve something real, with a STEM angle that goes deeper than a surface-level chatbot wrapper.

Strong Project Examples

  • A machine learning model for early detection of a local environmental hazard.
  • An AI tutoring assistant tailored for an underserved student population.
  • A natural language tool that translates dense medical or civic information into accessible language.
  • A computer vision system for community safety or agriculture monitoring.
  • A data analysis tool that helps a local nonprofit understand and serve its constituents better.

Submission

Your video demo is the main artifact judges evaluate.

Every team submits a 3-minute maximum video demo hosted on YouTube, Loom, or Google Drive. Unlisted links are fine.

  • Video demo, 3 minutes maximum.
  • Public GitHub repository at submission time.
  • Short write-up, 200-350 words.
  • Team roster with names, grade/year, school, and track selection.

Write-Up Questions

  • Who is the community you are helping?
  • What specific problem does your project address?
  • How does the AI component work?
  • What STEM domain does it engage in?
  • What are the biggest limitations right now?

Video Guide

  • Open with a clear 15-second problem statement.
  • Show the working product and AI in action.
  • Close with 30 seconds on impact and next steps.
  • Screen recordings with voiceover are welcome.
  • Captions or subtitles are encouraged.

Rules + Eligibility

Who can compete and what every team needs to know.

  • Open to current high school and undergraduate students.
  • Teams may have 1, 2, or 3 members.
  • Entry fee is $5 per person, paid at registration.
  • Projects must be started on or after May 15, 2026.
  • Open-source libraries and public APIs are allowed and must be credited.
  • An AI component and a real STEM domain are required.
  • Judges and organizers may not compete.
