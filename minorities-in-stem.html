Our Mission

An Initiative to Bridge the Gap in STEM

Minorities in STEM was founded in 2024 at Woodward Academy to bridge the gap between minorities and STEM. Acknowledging many initiatives to become more diverse and inclusive in the fields of robotics and technology, it became its primary focus and vision for STEM to become more inclusive to everyone in our community of STEM.

We support initiatives such as facilitating conversation within our community, bringing in guest speakers, and getting input from people in countless fields and backgrounds. Our goal is to raise awareness of the immense potential these students possess and help them see and believe in their own capabilities.

What We Do

Action that feels local and grows nationally.

Bridging the Gap

We make STEM feel reachable by connecting students with role models, resources, and practical experiences before opportunity starts to feel reserved for someone else. Our work is built around early access, consistent encouragement, and visible pathways.

Community

Join a Community That Grows With You

Creating a community of students in STEM to help each other go beyond the horizon. Whether you're just curious or already deep in research, you belong here.

We pair student energy with clear systems: chapters, events, mentorship, and shared resources that make it easier to start and harder to stop.

  • Peer support from students navigating similar STEM paths
  • Chapter programming that can adapt to local needs
  • Early exposure to STEM fields, projects, and mentors
  • A grounded place to ask questions and build confidence
